Red Thai Curry

Description

t's a red thai curry. It's hot. It tastes good. Not quite sure how much you need to know. Oh, so if you're expecting me to be making the paste from absolute scratch? Nah: we're good. Get a jar and make life easier on yourself. Oh and same for the extra additions. Get the lazy squeezy tubes.

Ingredients

  • 1 jar of Blue Dragon Red Thai Curry Paste
  • 1 tin of coconut milk
  • 1 chicken stock cube
  • 350ml water
  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 1 red pepper
  • 1 red onion
  • Handful of baby sweetcorn
  • Handful of mangetout
  • 1 tube of lemon grass
  • 1 tube of ginger
  • 1 tube of garlic paste
  • 1 bag or box of jasmine rice
  • 1 packet paraithas

Steps

  1. Thinly slice the chicken and place to one side.
  2. Thinly chop the pepper, onion, and baby sweetcorn. Put them alongside the mangetout in another pile.
  3. Dump around a third of the curry paste into a work along with a splash of olive oil and slowly heat.
  4. After a couple of minutes, throw in the chicken. Stir and allow to simmer for a few minutes.
  5. Boil the water and dissolve the chicken stock in a jug.
  6. Add the stock and coconut milk to the wok.
  7. Throw the vegetables in.
  8. Stir until everything is blended and then eyeball-squirt the lemon grass, ginger and garlic paste (just guesstimate, it won't kill you). Bring to a simmer and allow for the sauce to cook down. This usually take between 45-60 minutes.
  9. Cook the rice like it says on the packet. What am I, your mum? When the curry has cooked down enough, whack it on the lowest heat possible and fry the paraithas. Flip them until the begin to brown off and stick em on a plate.
  10. Serve up the rice and curry in a bowl and eat the stuff.
